Access To Finance

Guide to Accessing Credit In Oyo State

This guide provides information on various ways for residents and businesses in Oyo State to access credit, ranging from commercial banking to government programs and cooperative societies.

1. Formal Banking Institutions


  • Commercial Banks: Banks like First Bank, Access Bank, and Zenith Bank operate extensively in Oyo State, offering personal, SME, and agricultural loans. These require a good credit history, verifiable income, and, in some cases, collateral.

  • Application Process: Open an account, maintain a banking relationship, and provide documentation such as a business plan and collateral (where applicable). Certain banks also offer government-backed loans with favorable terms for priority sectors.

2. Microfinance Banks and Cooperative Societies


  • Microfinance Banks (MFBs): Oyo State hosts various MFBs like Seedvest MFB, which cater to smaller business loans with flexible terms. These are ideal for artisans, small-scale traders, and farmers.

  • Cooperative Societies: Cooperatives such as the Oyo State Cooperative Federation offer low-interest loans to members. Membership involves contributing to a shared fund from which loans are provided.

  • Application Process: MFBs require identification, proof of income, and sometimes guarantors. Cooperative members build a savings history before qualifying for credit.

3. Agricultural Credit Schemes


  • CBN’s Anchor Borrowers’ Programme (ABP): This program supports smallholder farmers by providing inputs or cash at subsidized interest rates, especially for cassava and maize farming.

  • Bank of Agriculture (BOA): The BOA offers credit for agricultural activities, from crop cultivation to processing. Oyo State’s Ministry of Agriculture can assist residents in accessing these funds.

  • Application Process: Farmers join cooperatives, provide land ownership or lease agreements, and guarantors. Some loans offer grace periods to accommodate seasonal agricultural needs.

4. SME Support Initiatives


  • Bank of Industry (BOI) SME Loans: BOI loans support SMEs in sectors such as manufacturing and ICT with low-interest rates, extended terms, and business development support.

  • National Economic Reconstruction Fund (NERFUND): NERFUND loans offer favorable terms for projects that drive economic growth and employment in the region.

  • Application Process: SMEs submit business plans, demonstrate viability, and provide collateral. Oyo State’s Ministry of Commerce and Industry can provide guidance on eligibility and application.

5. Government-Backed Credit Programs


  • Oyo State Government Programs: The Oyo State government has initiated several credit schemes to support local businesses through agencies like OYOMEDA.

  • National Youth Investment Fund (NYIF): The NYIF offers low-interest loans to young entrepreneurs aged 18-35 with amounts up to ₦3 million and rates around 5% per annum.

  • Application Process: Applicants need to be residents of Oyo State and often participate in business training programs to qualify for these government-supported loans.

6. Digital and Fintech Lending Platforms


  • Fintech Platforms: Apps like Carbon, FairMoney, and Branch provide quick, short-term loans without collateral. Interest rates are generally higher but ideal for immediate cash flow needs.

  • Application Process: Users download the app, register, and link their bank accounts. Loan disbursement is usually instant, though larger amounts may require a repayment history.

Contact Information for Additional Guidance

For more information, individuals can contact the following:


  • Ministry of Trade, Industry, Investment and Cooperatives Provides support for SME loans and business funding.

  • Agricultural Credit Corporation of Oyo State (ACCOS) Assists with agriculture-related loans and funding.

  • Microfinance Banks and Cooperatives: Many local cooperatives and MFBs provide flexible credit options.
